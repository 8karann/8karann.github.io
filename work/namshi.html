Namshi: a TikTok-first playbook for GCC fashion e-commerce

Namshi is the Middle East's leading online fashion retailer — 800+ brands, mobile-first, beloved by Gen Z shoppers from Riyadh to Dubai. This MBA strategy project built its next social media playbook: who to target, where to spend, what to post, and how to measure it.

Type
MBA Strategy Project — Digital Marketing
Role
Strategy & Research (team of 2)
Scope
Personas · Platform audit · Paid social · Content strategy
Market
GCC — Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain
01 — Audience

Personas built from behaviour

The target market — fashion-conscious 18–35s in the GCC, predominantly female, middle to upper-middle income, heavily influenced by social trends — was translated into two working personas that guided every downstream decision:

L

Layla, 26

Urban professional — Dubai, UAE
Behaviour
Active on Instagram; buys trendy outfits monthly; shops on mobile between meetings.
Goals
Stay stylish, discover new brands before her feed does.
Pain points
Limited time, overwhelmed by choice — needs curation, not catalogues.
How we win her
Influencer Reels, styled edits, retargeting with curated carousels.
O

Omar, 22

University student — Riyadh, KSA
Behaviour
Sneaker enthusiast; buys streetwear and sportswear; lives on TikTok and YouTube.
Goals
Express identity through drops and limited releases.
Pain points
Price sensitivity; distrusts polished brand ads — trusts creators.
How we win him
TikTok hashtag challenges, UGC, micro-influencer sneaker content.
02 — Platform Audit

Follow the engagement, not the follower count

Auditing Namshi's existing channels made the platform decision for us. TikTok's engagement rate was 5× Instagram's and roughly 17× Facebook/X — while most posting effort was still going to legacy channels.

Decision: TikTok becomes the primary growth channel (Gen Z reach, viral mechanics), Instagram the secondary brand channel (lifestyle, influencers), while Facebook/X are repositioned for paid reach and customer service only.

Influencer selection followed the same persona logic: a micro-influencer (UAE fashion blogger, 50K, high engagement, relatable) for authenticity, paired with a macro-influencer (Saudi lifestyle creator, 300K) for regional male-fashion reach.

05 — Paid & Search

Budget follows the journey

Paid social splits by job-to-be-done: Instagram (40% of paid budget) drives traffic and conversions with carousels, Reels and Story ads against fashion interests, lookalikes and site retargeting. TikTok takes awareness — in-feed video and branded hashtag challenges, timed to evening and weekend engagement peaks, where its CPA for Gen Z ran cheapest.
